## What to build

The comparison (tickets 01–07) shipped no model — `forecast.py` still runs the
equal-weight seasonal-naive average from the pilot. This ticket moves the two
winners into it, so the baker actually gets the numbers the experiment says are
better:

- **Poolish total (lead ~3):** replace the incumbent point model with
  `model_comparison.ewma_forecast` (same-weekday mean, 3-week half-life decay),
  and emit its 95% quantity via the uniform relative-residual buffer
  (`model_comparison.p95_buffer`) rather than a bare point forecast.
- **Bake split (lead ~2):** emit a Bake-to Quantity per variety by allocating
  that Poolish across `everything` / `plain` / `sesame` with
  `model_comparison.constant_recent_share`. No second buffer — the buffer lives
  once, in the total (ADR 0001).

`statsmodels` does **not** come with them: ETS scored best but did not earn its
dependency (below). It can stay in the `experiment` extra.

## Why this needs a human decision first

**The loss numbers do not compel the change; the dough numbers argue for it.**
Read `model_comparison.html` (generated by `inspection_page.py`) before deciding.

Over the 178 open days of 2026-01-09 → 2026-07-09, at the Poolish lead of 3 days:

| model                     | pinball@95 | coverage | leftover/day |
| ------------------------- | ---------: | -------: | -----------: |
| ets                       |      11.67 |    94.9% |          155 |
| **ewma**                  |  **12.06** | **94.9%** |      **161** |
| trailing_window           |      12.16 |    94.4% |          171 |
| seasonal_trend            |      12.62 |    98.3% |          195 |
| seasonal_naive (incumbent)|      12.67 |    98.3% |          196 |
| moving_average (baseline) |      21.19 |    94.9% |          362 |

- `ewma` beats the incumbent by **4.8%** on pinball@95 — but that is only **0.8
  standard errors** of the paired daily difference. On this much evidence the
  top seasonal models are **tied**; a lower mean is not a mandate.
- The seasonal models do beat the pilot's `moving_average` baseline decisively
  (43.1%, t = 3.4). That part of the PRD's question is settled.
- **ETS does not earn `statsmodels`**: 3.2% over `ewma` at t = 1.8, inside the
  noise, for a per-day model fit and a permanent production dependency.
- The real difference is **calibration**: the incumbent covers Demand on 98.3%
  of days when the shop asked for 95%, and pays for that unrequested safety with
  ~196 bagels' worth of leftover dough a day. `ewma` lands on 94.9% — the Service
  Level actually chosen — on ~161 leftover, i.e. **~36 fewer bagels of Poolish to
  make every morning** for the promised stockout rate (9 short days in 178, vs 3).

So the decision to make is: *do we accept trading 6 more short days per half-year
for ~36 fewer bagels of dough per day, which is exactly the 95% Service Level we
set?* If yes, promote. If the owner would rather keep over-baking, close this
`wontfix` and record that the 95% target is not what the shop actually wants.
