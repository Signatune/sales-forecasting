ewma — but tied with the incumbent

Same-weekday mean, 3-week half-life decay — the best-scoring Poolish model, though its 4.8% margin over seasonal_naive is inside the daily noise, and constant_recent_share for the bake split.

The Poolish models are tied on the evidence — no forced change. ewma does score the lowest pinball@95 of the dependency-free candidates, 4.8% under seasonal_naive, but that gap is only 0.8 standard errors of the paired daily difference — well inside the day-to-day noise on 178 days. Read honestly, this comparison cannot tell the top seasonal models apart, and a lower mean alone is not a mandate to swap out a model that already runs.

The seasonal models do earn their complexity. Against the pilot's moving_average baseline the winner cuts pinball@95 by 43.1%, at 3.4 standard errors — past the bar. A trailing daily mean smears the weekend peak across the week, and its P95 has to carry 362 bagels' worth of leftover dough a day to cover Saturday anyway.

ETS does not earn its dependency. It is the best scorer outright, but its edge over ewma is 3.2% at only 1.8 standard errors — the two are tied on this much evidence. A dependency the production path carries forever, plus a per-day model fit, has to be paid for with a real margin, and this is not one. statsmodels stays out: the PRD's open question, answered.

What actually separates them is dough, not loss. seasonal_naive covers Demand on 98.3% of days — it overshoots the 95% it was asked for, and pays for the extra safety with 196 bagels' worth of leftover dough a day (3 short days in 178). ewma lands on 94.9%, which is the Service Level the shop actually chose, and gets there on 161 leftover a day (9 short days) — about 36 fewer bagels of Poolish to make every morning. That, and not the pinball column, is the case for promoting it: the same promise, honestly priced, on less dough.

The bake split: constant_recent_share. It scores a mean WAPE of 7.7% across the three varieties, 6.1% below the runner-up. The mix is stable — the split is very nearly a non-race, exactly as the PRD suspected — so the simplest method that tracks the recent mix is enough, and the choice here is worth far less than the choice of Poolish model.

Nothing ships from this page. Promoting the winners — ewma for the Poolish total, constant_recent_share for the split — into forecast.py is ticket 08, and it is a decision for a human: the loss numbers do not compel it, the dough numbers argue for it.

How these scores are kept honest

Leak-free

Forecast from the past only

Every scored day is forecast from a 3-day-back origin via history_before, so no model ever sees the day it is judged on.

Same buffer

Every point forecast → P95 identically

Each candidate emits a point forecast; the evaluator applies the one uniform relative-residual buffer, so pinball measures forecast quality, not interval machinery.

Separate window

The buffer never sees its test days

Buffer residuals come from the 26 weeks strictly before the evaluation window — no scored day feeds its own P95.

By rule

The verdict is arithmetic

Margins are tested, not admired: two models' daily losses are paired day by day, and a gap counts only past 2 standard errors. The prose below renders that rule — it does not reach around it.
